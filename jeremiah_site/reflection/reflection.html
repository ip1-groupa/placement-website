Application process for Placement

This particular placement opportunity was extended to me by the Career Development at my institution, the African Leadership University, Mauritius.

I was sent the Job description and application requirements document by email. I wrote up a cover letter and submitted it - along with my resume - to the email indicated on the requirements document. I received a reply from Paga's HR team with a date for my interview. I was well prepared for my interview when it came around and I received an official offer four days later.

I accepted the offer and took up the job once I returned to Nigeria.

Job Details

As a Product Manager at Paga, I was assigned to a 5-man unit, concentrating primarily on the overall project of managing the Paga app V2 release. My key reponsibilities can be summarized as:

● Map back-office process directly linked to existing Paga products to identify areas of improvement and optimisation
● Carry out research, assess and recommend systems or tools to support internal teams and departments
● Work closely with product managers to design user journey and experiences across a number of channels including mobile, web and USSD
● Assist with tasks related to identifying, defining and validating user problems

Placement Outcome Evaluation

I’ve had a truly phenomenal internship experience. I had a few downs during my time at Paga, but it was a huge up overall. It was sort of difficult settling into the office at first because I lived far away, and it was tedious navigating Lagos traffic to get to work and back every day. I came up with a strong plan to battle that problem and life became a lot better.
My internship at Paga was very hands-on and process oriented and I loved that. I was given full control of a lot of the tasks I was assigned and my regular check-ins with my manager helped orient me on the right way to go about many tasks. I learned about process optimization, UI/UX design and front-end development. It was great to be so integrated into a company and learn about its processes and structures and make actionable impact.
This placement was a huge success for me and I couldn’t have wished for a better outcome.

Lessons Learnt

This placement was an enlightening experience. A lot of things went even better than I expected. But there were some lessons I learnt that I wish I’d learned earlier.

If I had the opportunity to go through this placement again, I would place more emphasis on the culture of asking questions and being proactive in seeking help. I know I’d have made less mistakes if I asked more questions.

A second chance at this placement would’ve awarded me an opportunity to take on a wider range of tasks and truly diversify my skillset.

My advice to you

My advice to any individual taking on an internship? Give it your all.

Work on getting a placement at an organization whose culture and identity fall in line with your interests. It will never feel like a job if you love what you’re doing and you’re having fun doing it. Put your effort into what ever tasks you’re assigned. Ask questions on things you don’t understand and report back to your manager regularly. Don’t be afraid to take on tasks outside your comfort zone and learn new things.

The most important thing is to make sure that you’re constantly creating value for company and yourself.
